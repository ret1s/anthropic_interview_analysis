## Workforce themes


# Themes from Interview Transcripts

## 1. AI as a Time-Saving Tool
- **Example:** A funeral director uses AI to write obituaries, saving time and ensuring character limits for newspaper publications.
- **Example:** A pastor uses AI to generate email templates and draft vision documents, freeing up time for pastoral care.

## 2. AI in Creative and Educational Processes
- **Example:** A high school teacher uses AI for brainstorming lesson ideas, finding it helpful for generating engaging activities.
- **Example:** A university lecturer uses AI to gain new perspectives on teaching complex topics like the Harlem Renaissance.

## 3. Challenges and Limitations of AI
- **Example:** A software developer experiences frustration when AI gets stuck in debugging loops, requiring manual intervention.
- **Example:** A videographer finds AI's generative fill tool sometimes produces "odd or unexpected" results, necessitating manual cleanup.

## 4. Ethical and Professional Considerations
- **Example:** A university lecturer is cautious about openly discussing AI use due to concerns about academic integrity and perceptions of "cheating."
- **Example:** A pastor emphasizes the importance of maintaining boundaries with AI to ensure it remains a tool rather than a replacement for human connection.

## 5. Workplace Dynamics and AI Adoption
- **Example:** A software developer was initially reluctant to use AI until encouraged by a manager and seeing coworkers' productivity gains.
- **Example:** A teacher finds herself educating colleagues about AI, while institutional policies are still being developed to address its use in education.

## Creatives themes


# Themes from Creative Professionals' Use of AI

## 1. AI as a Creative Catalyst
- **Example:** A musician uses AI plugins like Scaler and Orb Producer to overcome writer's block by generating chord progressions and musical ideas.
- **Example:** A graphic designer uses AI to extend image backgrounds and adjust elements, allowing for more creative freedom in layout design.

## 2. Maintaining Creative Control
- **Example:** An illustrator avoids using generative AI for core creative work, emphasizing the importance of exercising creativity as a muscle.
- **Example:** A game designer uses AI to identify deeper connections between writing and design but ensures they drive the creative decisions.

## 3. AI for Efficiency and Time-Saving
- **Example:** A real estate photographer uses AI to quickly edit photos, removing unwanted objects and enhancing images, significantly reducing editing time.
- **Example:** A graphic designer finds AI invaluable for quickly manipulating images to fit design needs, freeing time for more creative tasks.

## 4. Concerns About Job Security and Industry Impact
- **Example:** A musician worries about AI replacing human jobs in creative fields, as companies might opt for cheaper AI solutions.
- **Example:** A writer fears AI-generated content flooding markets, potentially overshadowing thoughtful, boundary-pushing work.

## 5. AI as a Strategic and Business Tool
- **Example:** An illustrator uses AI to craft pitches and client communications, allowing more focus on creative projects.
- **Example:** A performance manager uses AI to refine writing for marketing materials, enhancing clarity and engagement.
